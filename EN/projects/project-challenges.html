Project Challenges

I have faced many challenges during the several responsibilities I have held. I have the ability to successfully handle various types of challenges, including technical challenges that require learning new skills in a short period of time, as well as challenges related to the team I lead and the client we work for. Below are examples of the major challenges I faced and solved.

Mirodec — Lead Designer

Lost Design Data — Schindler, KSA

The company lost data related to designs that should be printed for one of our major clients, Schindler in the KSA.

Action Taken

I created new designs that were close to the samples the client agreed on.

Resolution

I requested a call with the client and convinced him to approve the new design.

Mirodec — Lead Designer

Self-Learning HP Glass Printing Machine & Software

When I was first hired at Mirodec, I had to learn on my own how to use the HP glass printing machine and the related software because it was a new industry that I hadn't worked in before, and the designer before me left without giving a notice period.

Action Taken

Self-taught the HP glass printing machine operation and its software with zero handover from the previous designer. Learned the end-to-end production workflow independently while continuing to deliver on ongoing client projects.

Outcome

Became fully proficient in glass printing operations and software within a short period, ensuring uninterrupted production for all active client orders.

Dataflow — Design Team Leader

Qatar Ministry of Education — Scaling from 5 to 25 Designers

The company received a huge project for the Qatar Ministry of Education. The project required very fast delivery and a big team of designers — at least 25 — while my team had only 5 designers. The company's initial plan was to rely on outsourcing because we didn't have enough time to hire and train new designers. However, the first delivery was a complete disaster because we didn't have control over the quality of the outsourced work and the client was unhappy.

Action Taken

I prepared training materials and a plan to recruit new designers instead of outsourcing. I volunteered to make the plan succeed and worked 16–20 hours per day for 3 months to complete my daily tasks, interview candidates, and train new designers.

Outcome

We successfully delivered the project. The team grew from 5 to 25 designers. I was promoted to Project Manager as a result of this effort.

Seven Seas Cards — Project Manager

KYC Provider Change — Regulatory Compliance

The original KYC (Know Your Customer) provider was replaced before launch due to updated regulatory requirements. The initially selected provider was found to be non-compliant with new standards introduced shortly before go-live.

Impact

Required rework of the identity verification flow — API integration, document upload, liveness detection, and backend verification pipelines had to be re-tested end to end. Delayed the onboarding module by approximately 2 weeks.

Resolution

Fast-tracked vendor evaluation; parallel-tracked integration and compliance review. New provider was fully integrated and passed UAT before launch deadline.

Seven Seas Cards — Project Manager

Currency Exchange API — Rate Update Failure

On the Friday before our weekend, the third-party API responsible for currency exchange rates stopped sending updated rates without warning. The exchange rate API began returning stale/cached rates. FX conversions in the app showed incorrect amounts for multi-currency cardholders. Detected late Friday afternoon.

Impact

Risk of cardholders seeing incorrect balances and making financial decisions based on wrong data. Required immediate escalation to the provider and implementation of a fallback mechanism before the weekend.

Resolution

Implemented a fallback rate source (secondary provider) with automatic failover. Added monitoring alerts for rate staleness. Deployed hotfix Friday evening before weekend blackout.

Seven Seas Cards — Project Manager

Vendor Coordination Across Time Zones

Multiple third-party providers — card processor, KYC, FX, SMS gateway — operating in different time zones created significant coordination overhead.

Impact

Issue resolution often delayed by 12–24 hours due to time zone gaps. Required careful scheduling of integration windows and escalation paths.

Resolution

Established clear SLAs with each vendor specifying response times. Created a shared escalation matrix. Scheduled overlapping support windows for critical integration phases.

Seven Seas Cards — Project Manager

Regulatory Compliance Timeline

Evolving regulatory landscape required mid-project adjustments to compliance features — transaction monitoring thresholds, KYC tier requirements, and reporting formats changed during development.

Impact

Required rework of already-completed features and continuous alignment with the compliance team. Risked scope creep and timeline delays.

Resolution

Embedded compliance review into the sprint cadence. Maintained a living compliance requirements tracker. Prioritized regulatory items alongside feature development to avoid last-minute rework.

Seven Seas Cards — Project Manager

Device Registration & Security

Implementing device binding and authorization added complexity to the onboarding flow. Balancing security requirements with user experience required multiple design iterations.

Impact

Initial designs created friction in the onboarding flow — users dropped off at the device authorization step. Security team requirements conflicted with UX goals.

Resolution

Ran multiple design iterations with UX and security teams. Implemented progressive security — basic binding during onboarding, enhanced checks for sensitive operations. Drop-off rate reduced while maintaining security compliance.

Seven Seas Cards — Project Manager

Notification System Complexity

Push and in-app notifications for 12+ event types — refund, reversal, POS, PIN change, currency exchange, card status, e-commerce, magnetic stripe, and all transaction types — required extensive test case coverage and edge-case handling.

Impact

High risk of missed or duplicate notifications. Complex state management across notification channels. Coordination between backend, mobile, and SMS gateway teams.

Resolution

Built a comprehensive test case matrix covering all event type × channel combinations. Implemented idempotency keys to prevent duplicate notifications. Created a notification preferences engine giving users granular control.

Seven Seas Cards — Project Manager

Multi-Currency Edge Cases

Handling transactions across currencies introduced rounding discrepancies, rate timing issues, and display inconsistencies.

Impact

Cardholders occasionally saw mismatched amounts between transaction history and balance displays. Required careful business logic for FX conversion timing.

Resolution

Standardized FX rate sourcing to a single provider per transaction. Implemented consistent rounding rules across all display surfaces. Added automated reconciliation checks to flag discrepancies.
